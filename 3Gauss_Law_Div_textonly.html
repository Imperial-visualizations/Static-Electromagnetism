Maxwell's second equation states that; $$ \nabla \cdot \mathbf{B} = 0$$ Where $\nabla$ is the divergence operator. This means that magnetic fields are divergence free. But what does this actually mean?
A field is divergence free if the field is invariant along the path of the field lines. So, if one was to take an 'infinitesimal step' along a field line, then the field strength would not change.
