Maxwell's first law states that; $$ \nabla \cdot \mathbf{B} = \frac{\rho}{\epsilon_0}$$ Where $\nabla$ is the divergence operator. This law implies that electric charges exhibit divergence that is proportional to E-field density. But what does this actually mean?
A field is divergent if the field is varies along the path of the field lines. So, if one was to take an 'infinitesimal step' along a field line, then the field strength would change. In terms of electrostatics this means that electric charges are the sources of electric fields.
